Jodhpur:The Blue City

Jodhpur is also known as The Blue City because of all the houses being painted blue in the ancient times to keep the heat at bay. Jodhpur is also known as ‘Blue City’ as the houses are blue-painted in the old area of the city.

Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort,it's a well maintained and famous fort with an articulate museum inside it.Mehrangarh, located in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, is one of the largest forts in India. Built around 1459 by Rao Jodha, the fort is situated 410 feet above the city and is enclosed by imposing thick walls. Inside its boundaries there are several palaces known for their intricate carvings and expansive courtyards.

Jaswanthada

Jaswanthada,a beautiful white marble building with a well maintained garden.The Jaswant Thada is a cenotaph located in Jodhpur, in the Indian state of Rajasthan. It was built by Maharaja Sardar Singh of Jodhpur State in 1899 in memory of his father, Maharaja Jaswant Singh II,[1] and serves as the cremation ground for the royal Rajput family of Marwar.
